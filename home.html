About

Welcome fellow game enthusiast to this page! This is an informative site to give you a short overview of Generic Universal Role-Playing System or better know as GURPS and help to decide if this is a game system for you before you commit to it.

Our goal is to help new people to get into the tabletop role playing game (TTRPG) hobby, as well as people who have been in hobby a while and are looking to try out new game systems. Content of this page is focused on how to play GURPS 4th edition, but most principles apply to the older editions as well.

And finally for people that decide to give it a try at playing GURPS, point to useful online resources to help you start your first game.

What is GURPS?

GURPS is a generic role-paying system created by Steve Jackson Games to help people play any game setting. This system uses point buy mechanic to help players and game master (GM) build their characters to fit the concept and setting that the gaming group wants to play.

Unlike class based and levelling systems like Dungeons & Dragons, that have a set character path based on the class you chose such as wizard or a fighter, GURPS serves as a toolbox from which the players pick various traits approved by the GM and purchase them with character points.

This system is also setting agnostic, meaning it is easy to implement it to any type of game and setting you and your group want to play in. For example, you want to play a space opera fighting a evil crime syndicate that wants to overthrow the Galactic Federation, just to take few weeks after a refresher and play a classical medieval fantasy dungeon crawls, this system allows it.

Why GURPS?

Why GURPS?

GURPS is a generic roleplaying system that is independent of setting and style of play. Some settings have more focus on combat like Dungeons and Dragons, other implement storytelling system like World of Darkness games, and most systems have mechanics that are tied to a specific setting or type of setting (e.g. medieval fantasy, modern setting, space exploration).

Most systems have either a light abstract mechanic or a crunchy have mechanics that promote a lot of optimizations on the player side. There is nothing wrong with either of these approaches, but for beginner players who are not sure how they want to play their game or game masters that want to implement a cool new thing in the game without thinking will this make a game unplayable, this can represent a problem. GURPS presents to player both options, with in our experience easy transition from one style of play into the other, in case the group wants to change the way they play the current game. Low action economy in both the mechanics heavy and light version allows for a fast-paced game, where there is low waiting time between player turns.

Finally, play the game that you want! With this system the game can be focused on the combat heavy game, as well as a skill-based game that may not have any combat at all but instead focuses on investigation, exploration, intrigue and/or player focused world building.
